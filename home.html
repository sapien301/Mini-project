Karnataka is a land of ultimate natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. It is situated in south-west of India. The western coast is splased by Arabian sea with some heavenly beaches. Karnataka is blessed with the lush green Western Ghats, roaring Jog waterfalls, densely forested Sahayadri, and the rich wildlife sanctuaries.
Karnataka is truly a state with many worlds.

Explore Karnataka

The Mysore Palace


Once the residence of the Wodeyars,
is one of the largest palaces of its kind in India,
and one of the most splendid. Built in Indo-Saracenic
style with domes,turrets, arches and colonnades.

Mysore Palace Mysore

Kudremukh Peak


At an altitude of 1894 m above sea level,
the Kudremukh Peak is a paradise for trekkers
and naturists alike, with its mountainous paths
and floral and faunal diversity.

Kudremukh,Chikkamagaluru

Jog Falls


The Western Ghats gives rise to the Sharavati
river that plunges from a height of 829 feet in
four magnificent cascades namely Raja, Rani,
Roarer and Rocket.
Jog falls, being the tallest waterfalls
in India is situated in the dense evergreen forests.

Jog Falls,Shivamogga

Mirjan Fort


Located on the banks of River Aghanashini,
Mirjan Fort is a royal reminder of our
glorious history.The splendid fort truly
gives the impression of a fairytale castle all
with grass and overgrowth.

Mirjan Fort,Uttara Kannada
